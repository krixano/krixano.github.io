Explanation

The first allusion I make is to my poem "The People, Their Missions". The original lines from this poem are as follows: 

The people their missions
their clueless sickness
that spreads the world

"The People, Their Missions" is about being overwhelmed with emotions from all the bad that happens within the world, and wanting to find some way to block it all out and not have to feel anymore. I was struggling with recurring thoughts about all the evil within the world, not able to forget. I think it is likely this poem was written before every other poem of mine that I allude to here, so this is the start of me trying to block everything out.

The next poem I allude to is "Superficial". Some of the main ideas behind this poem was to show hidden emotions, being misunderstood, and a lowering of self-esteem and confidence as time goes on (the yellowing/"lost color" and the peeling back of the onion), yet also trying, and failing, to stand up for oneself (the lengthening of each subsequent stanza).

Onions generally make your eyes tear up. These are fake ("artificial") tears because they have no emotion behind them. These fake tears are in response to the "cursing", a means of protecting oneself when their protection has been peeled off. "Regardless of the benefits" represents the message of this "cursing". The stinging in your eyes and the cursing from the onion, while seemingly painful, have a message, a message that has deliberately not been heard.

The next stanza gives a clue that this onion is really a person who has feelings, yet feel like they cannot show it ("yet shown not"). Feelings are inward, and the inability to show them, masking oneself, is outward. Two "must"s follow, an inward must and an outward must, following in the same pattern as described above. The next line explains that what appears to be selfish (an outward selfish) is actually selfless (an inward selfless). You'll see this pattern continue in the next stanzas.

The idea of absorbing illnesses comes back. The onion takes all of the pain from the outer world, and from what could be considered home ("origin waters") and absorbes it. This is the cause of the lost color (yellowing) from before. This pain has a lasting effect, even though the outward image remains ("truly remains") stable, superficial ("truly superficial").

The next allusion made is to my poem "Everything and Nothing", which is about having thoughts of not belonging in the world and not wanting to be in it. It also talks about how certain relationships have only caused pain, making the person angry, not themselves, and increasing the desire to not want to feel anymore. Yet while not wanting to feel anymore, the feelings and pain never really stop.

The allusion to Questioning was made because of thoughts that I didn't belong in the world because I was different. Thoughts of being scared to tell anyone about this for fear of being ostracized or misunderstood. But also, questions about why I am the way that I am and whether I was a bad person for that.

The line "My dream hesitation" comes from the poem "Behind Out" and, which isn't on the website due to how personal it is. "Behind Out" is a poem about my feelings while writing my poem "Out", another very personal poem that hasn't been added to the site yet. Behind Out deals with questions about how I didn't choose the way that I am, to feel horrible and afraid everyday for the way that I am, and questions about how a God could not accept me for something that I couldn't control, yet wanting a longing personal connection with God very badly. This leads into the next stanza very well.

The second stanza takes the message of the song "Broken" by Lifehouse, and turns part of the message. "Broken" is about holding on to God even when you have doubts and pain in your life. It's about finding God healing you even when you are at your lowest point in life and don't want to be healed.

The first 4 lines of this stanza refer to this part of the lyrics of the song: 

The broken clock is a comfort
It let's me sleep tonight
Maybe it can stop tomorrow
From stealing all my time

The idea is that I wanted time to stop, for life to stop moving, to be comforted by complete pause of the constant pain and suffering that life brings - "a broken clock / a comfort stuck". Yet, the clock would never break for me. The line "No reflections" refers to these lines in the song: 

I still see your reflection
Inside my eyes
That are looking for purpose
There still looking for life

These lines are talking about God. In this time in my life I couldn't see how there could be a God. Everything about the world showed me that either a God couldn't exist, or that God was cruel. The last line of this stanza is me holding on as much as I can without anyone there, especially God ("Name" is capitalized).

The next stanza is about my social anxiety, about not being able to freely express myself, about not being comfortable with communicating with people, about being afraid of this and of generally just being out in the world, of being known and noticed. This is part of the reason why I was so locked up in myself. I couldn't get comfort from anywhere else because of these fears.
